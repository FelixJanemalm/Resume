Pitching to RedBullTV

Campaign Pitch - RedBullTV

Berghs Bachelor

redbulltv.com

A great benefit with studying at Berghs School of Communication is that you work on real client cases. During the fall of 2016, my group pitched our "Free Thrills" Campaign to RedBullTv.

RedBullTV is a streaming platform that offers a range of genres in video content. The challenges we faced were that the platform had close to zero recognition and we proved through interviews that people were very fast to assume we were advertising the RedBull drink, so our message was easily lost. We understood that people who did not like the RedBull drink would not listen to the message and less tech-savvy audiences would not yield a high conversion ratio.

Our strategy was to hone in on the younger part of our demographic who were already brand fans and get them to evangelize for us. To them, the recognition would be a strength. So throughout our campaign, we focused on the content on the platform which was closest to the RedBull brand. Our messaging was aimed at driving signups from the younger audience and used references to other streaming platforms to put our message in the right context. We made sure to mention that the platform was free, which was especially relevant to the lower income segment we directed our campaign towards.

Electric Skateboard Build

For me, this was one of those moments where you realize you can do anything you set your mind to, with some help from the internet.

I had no prior experience from working with electronics when I saw Boosted Boards Ted talk, pitching their early concept electric powered skateboard. Why would you say no to “these” benefits?

With a Bachelor in marketing and browsing future outlooks, I think the benefits of agile marketing are apparent.

There is a lot happening within agile marketing at the moment. Companies willing to invest in the marketing tools, people and processes needed to make it work are breaking new ground. As agile marketing matures we will learn a lot about what it can and can’t do.

To me, this is the most interesting change that is happening in advertising now. To be honest, I never planned to “not be” on the product development team, which my previous engineering education is a testament to. I want to be and push, the change I want to see within my industry.

Therefore, I am writing a book.

A Practical Guide to Agile Marketing.

“Half the money I spend on advertising is wasted; the trouble is I don't know which half.” John Wanamaker (1838-1922) was a very successful United States merchant, religious leader and political figure, considered by some to be a "pioneer in marketing".

It is soon to be one hundred years since John past and a lot has changed since then. With more data than ever to inform our decisions, we no longer have to guess which half of the marketing budget is well spent.

Since the 20th century begun, business managers have come a long way to reduce waste in their operations. I think it is time the marketers caught up; The Tv-Industrial Complex is out and actionable metrics are in.
